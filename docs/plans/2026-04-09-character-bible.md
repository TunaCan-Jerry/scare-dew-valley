# Scaredew Valley — Character Bible

## Archetype System

Every character has a **primary pillar** and a **secondary pillar**. The combination defines their archetype.

### Five Pillars

- **Fighter** — people who are useful when things get violent
- **Fixer** — people who build, repair, and keep things running
- **Community** — people who hold the group together
- **Knowledgeable** — people who understand things
- **Weird** — people with unusual or unexplained abilities/perspectives

### Archetype Matrix (15 combinations)

|  | Fighter | Fixer | Community | Knowledge | Weird |
|--|---------|-------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| **Fighter** | Warrior | Armorer | Sensei | Tactician | Berserker |

## Designed Characters

### 1. Rem (placeholder name) — Scholar (Knowledge + Knowledge)

- **Age:** Mid-to-late 20s
- **Gender:** Female
- **Personality:** Autistic. Shy and reserved by default. Lights up when you hit a special interest — animated, fast-talking, brilliant. Infodumps without noticing when people zone out.
- **Pre-apocalypse:** Grad student who kept switching programs because everything was fascinating. Never finished a degree but knows more than most professors.
- **Arrival:** Found on an excursion, holed up in a library surrounded by cross-referenced book stacks.
- **Trait:** Polymath — unlocks research paths based on 3 randomized academic interests.
- **Special mechanic — Academic Interests:** Each run, the seed picks 3 from a pool of 12. These determine her research unlocks and gameplay bonuses.

### 2. Everett — Occultist (Knowledge + Weird)

- **Age:** Early 30s
- **Gender:** Male
- **Personality:** Sardonic, intense, talks too fast when excited. Dark humor. Oscillates between "I told you so" smugness and genuine guilt that he couldn't warn anyone. Underneath the cynicism, deeply afraid — he knows how bad this really is.
- **Pre-apocalypse:** Adjunct professor of folklore and comparative mythology. Published papers on "practical applications of apotropaic traditions" — got laughed out of conferences. Tiny apartment full of books and eBay occult artifacts. No tenure, no funding, no respect.
- **Arrival:** If seed includes him, shows up Day 1 before dusk, frantic, warning the player about what's coming tonight. Proven right by nightfall.
- **Trait:** Mythos Expert — identifies enemy types/weaknesses after first encounter. Unlocks bestiary. Faster Arcane Study research.
- **Heart events:** H2: shows obsessive coffee-stained notes. H4: admits he hid during the first night, ashamed. H6: you find his published paper on excursion — he gets emotional reading reviews that called him delusional. H8: confesses the apocalypse might partially be his fault — his "theoretical" ritual was used as a blueprint. H10: performs a protective ward on the farm. It works. He cries.
- **Max-bond ability:** Forbidden Knowledge — once per night, choose one enemy type, all defenses deal double damage to that type.
- **Weakness:** Physically useless. Panics under direct pressure. Wall defense liability. Nightmares suggest something is noticing him back.
- **Romanceable:** Yes
- **Synergy:** Research duo with Rem (bonus speed when both on Arcane Study).

### 3. Mabel — Occultist (Knowledge + Weird)

- **Age:** Late 60s
- **Gender:** Female
- **Personality:** Warm, unflappable, slightly amused by the apocalypse. Speaks plainly. Dry wit. Makes you tea while explaining the sigil on your doorframe saved your life. Keeps secrets out of habit.
- **Pre-apocalypse:** Lived alone on the edge of town. Grew strange herbs, kept cats, was "that weird old lady." Neighbors left casseroles, she left protective charms they thought were crafts. Warded her property for 40 years. Her wife was "the real talent" — she kept up the practice after she passed.
- **Arrival:** Found on excursion. Property intact, wards glowing. Agrees to come but packs three suitcases of herbs, bones, mysterious jars, and one very old cat.
- **Trait:** Wardcrafter — places protective wards on buildings. Warded buildings take reduced damage and repel non-corporeal enemies.
- **Heart events:** H2: gives you a small warm charm for your pocket. H4: teaches which plants have power. H6: tells about her late wife. H8: found performing midnight ritual — she's been containing something under the farm for decades. Exhausted. H10: teaches you the ward. "I'm glad someone else can carry it now."
- **Max-bond ability:** Greater Ward — once per night, unbreakable barrier on one wall section. Non-corporeal enemies blocked for 30 seconds.
- **Weakness:** Old. Slow. Can't do physical labor well. Needs rest. If overworked, wards weaken.
- **Romanceable:** No — deep grandmother friendship arc instead.
- **Synergy:** Teases Everett gently, he's awed. Patient with Rem's infodumps. Adopts them both.

### 4. Vess (Vessel) — Occultist (Knowledge + Weird)

- **Age:** Early 20s (21-23)
- **Gender:** Female
- **Visual:** Entire body below the neck covered in sigils, runes, and magic symbols. Covers up with long sleeves, high collars, gloves.
- **Personality:** Quiet. Watchful. Flat, precise sentences. Says unsettling things casually. Slowly learning sarcasm and humor — awkward endearing jokes. Fiercely independent. Flinches when people raise their voices.
- **Pre-apocalypse:** Born into doomsday cult ("The Congregation of the Veil" or similar). Raised performing rituals told were symbolic — they weren't. Groomed as vessel/conduit. Escaped weeks before the apocalypse. Cult completed ritual without her, imperfectly. That's why the apocalypse is this instead of something worse.
- **Arrival:** Found on excursion hiding in abandoned church surrounded by counter-sigils she drew. Containing a local breach alone.
- **Trait:** Insider Knowledge — advance wave prediction. Can perform rituals that weaken boss enemies. High risk, high reward.
- **Heart events:** H2: "Do you choose to be here?" She's never chosen anything. H4: draws a working sigil, stares at her hands. H6: tells about the cult, matter-of-fact. H8: someone says she's dangerous — how you handle it defines the relationship. H10: chooses a new name from seed-dependent pool.
- **Chosen name pool** (seed picks 2-3 to offer, some conditional on who's present):

| Name | Why | Conditional? |
|------|-----|-------------|
| June | "It's a month. Nobody owns it." | No |
| Maeve | Read in a book Rem lent her. | Rem in run |
| Robin | Watched robins nesting. First small alive thing. | No |
| Clara | Means "bright/clear." Opposite of her past. | No |
| Dani | Short for nothing. Just Dani. | No |
| Sage | Mabel's "sage advice" pun. First laugh. | Mabel in run |
| Lark | Morning bird. Wants to be a daytime thing. | No |
| Noor | Means "light." Heard from another character. | No |
| Ivy | "It grows on things and holds on." | No |
| Hazel | After Mabel's cat. | Mabel in run |

- **Max-bond ability:** Unraveling — targets boss, reduces HP 25%, disables one ability. Costs 1 heart level temporarily.
- **Weakness:** Traumatized. Prone to dissociation. Morale drop = shutdown. Others distrust her initially.
- **Romanceable:** Yes — slow, consent-focused arc about choice.

#### Boss Vess

- If Vess is NOT in the seed's recruitable pool, 80% chance she appears as a **mid-game boss**.
- The cult completed the ritual WITH her. She's the fully realized Vessel. Sigils glowing.
- Boss mechanics mirror her ally support kit — casts wards against YOU, weakens your defenses, debuffs your people.
- **Default outcome:** tragic kill. She's gone.
- **Secret rescue condition (extremely rare, community-discovery):**
  - Requires Mabel AND Everett both in the run
  - Specific combined arcane research completed
  - Possibly a specific excursion item
  - Non-obvious action during the boss fight (e.g., don't attack during a phase, use specific ability)
  - No in-game hints. Player must piece it together from dialogue and instinct.
- **If rescued:** Joins at Heart 0 with debuffs. Weaker than recruit-Vess. Different heart arc focused on recovery. H10 name moment hits even harder.

### 5. Rowan — Armorer (Fighter + Fixer)

- **Age:** Mid 30s to early 40s
- **Gender:** Male
- **Visual:** Big. Burly. Red hair and red beard. Largest person on the farm.
- **Personality:** Gentle giant. Soft-spoken — calm, thoughtful voice. Patient, works at his own pace. Takes pride in craftsmanship. Eats constantly, matter-of-fact about it. Laughs easily. Best hugs in the apocalypse.
- **Pre-apocalypse:** Ran a small forge — custom knives, tools, decorative ironwork. YouTube channel, 12k subscribers. Craft fairs. Cabin with a big workshop. Happiest man alive until the world ended.
- **Arrival:** Found on excursion in rural area. His wood-fired forge is still running. Reluctant to leave but comes if you set up a smithing area.
- **Trait:** Ironmonger — offensive structures +20% HP and +15% damage. Forges custom ammo without Armory. Carries/places heavy structures in half time.
- **Gameplay:** Turret/trap specialist. Converts scrap into higher-value materials. Food cost 1.5-2x normal — real tradeoff in lean runs. Solid wall defender at night through sheer strength.
- **Heart events:** H2: shows forge, apologizes for the state of it. H4: makes you something small and perfect. H6: photo on cracked phone — grinning in leather apron. Misses making beautiful things. H8: forges a wind chime or iron flower. "Place needs something that isn't about killing." H10: teaches forge basics. "Metal remembers every strike. So do people." Custom item.
- **Max-bond ability:** Forge Fire — once per night, superheats all metal defenses in area. Turrets deal fire damage, fences burn enemies, razor wire becomes lethal. One wave.
- **Weakness:** 1.5-2x food cost. Slow. Useless at delicate/technical work. Not a tactician.
- **Romanceable:** Yes — warm, steady, uncomplicated. You just feel safe.

### 6. Sal (Salvador) — Armorer (Fighter + Fixer)

- **Age:** Late 50s
- **Gender:** Male
- **Ethnicity:** Hispanic
- **Personality:** Tired but not defeated. Short sentences. Doesn't volunteer information. Has a routine and sticks to it. Shows care through gruff practicality, not words. Notices when people are struggling before they do.
- **Pre-apocalypse:** Army infantry, two tours in Afghanistan. Parents died at 7 in car accident, raised by loving grandparents. Came home with bad knee, worse sleep. Opened one-man auto shop. Divorced — she couldn't live with who he came back as. No kids. Was just existing.
- **Arrival:** Starter or early arrival. Drove up in beat-up truck loaded with tools. Didn't ask to stay, just started working on perimeter. "So are you staying?" "Looks like it."
- **Trait:** Veteran's Eye — repaired buildings restored to full HP. Calls out enemy positions, giving nearby defenders accuracy bonus. Reliable all-rounder.
- **Gameplay:** Versatile defender. Not specialized like Rowan but solid everywhere. Only character who can repair structures DURING night combat without ability. Never has a bad night.
- **Heart events:** H2: up before anyone, hands you coffee without a word. H4: fixes something you didn't ask about. "It was bothering me." H6: photo of ex-wife. "Some things you can't fix." H8: bad night, everyone shaken. He talks — really talks — about surviving things. Most words he's ever said. It helps. H10: gives you his dog tags. "I don't need 'em to remember."
- **Max-bond ability:** Hold The Line — once per night, one wall section becomes indestructible for one wave. He just stands there. Nothing gets through.
- **Weakness:** Bad knee (slow). PTSD — loud unexpected blasts cause brief freeze. Nightmares return when morale is low.
- **Romanceable:** Yes — quiet, complicated. Afraid of getting close again.
